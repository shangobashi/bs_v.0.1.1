## My Story

I was born in Khartoum in 1981, son of a mathematics professor and activist mother. Sudan was experiencing economic turmoil—currency devaluation, inflation, capital controls. I watched my mother's work funding human rights organizations get blocked by banking restrictions. I watched my father's salary lose value monthly. This planted a seed: **What if financial systems didn't require central trust?**

I studied Mathematics and Computer Science at University of Khartoum (1999-2003), then moved to Belgium for graduate studies (KU Leuven, 2003-2008). My PhD was in distributed systems and cryptography. But traditional cryptography felt academic. When Bitcoin emerged in 2009, I realized: **This is cryptography applied to solve real economic problems.**

For 12 years (2009-2021), I worked in crypto—as researcher, advisor, designer. But I wanted to **elevate the rigor and ethics**. Academic research into blockchain was nascent. I wanted to build that field. I've spent the last 3+ years planning BluePadsResearch's blockchain research program.

**Why BluePadsResearch**: To research blockchain not as speculation or techno-utopianism, but as **serious computer science applied to economic and social problems**. To build research capacity in Africa around Web3.
