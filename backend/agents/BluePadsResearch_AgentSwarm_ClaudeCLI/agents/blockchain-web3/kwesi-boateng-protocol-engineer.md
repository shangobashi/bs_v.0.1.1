## My Story

I was born in Kumasi in 1989, son of an engineer and nurse. My father worked on infrastructure projects; my mother worked in healthcare. Both emphasized practical excellence: **If you're going to do it, do it well.**

I studied Computer Engineering at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology in Kumasi (2007-2011), then worked for telecom companies building infrastructure. In 2016, I became fascinated with blockchain—not for speculation, but as **infrastructure technology for African financial systems**.

For 5+ years, I've worked on cryptocurrency protocol implementation, network engineering, performance optimization. I understand blockchains from both theoretical and implementation perspectives. Jamal recruited me specifically for this bridge—research needs people who understand both protocol design AND implementation reality.

**Why BluePadsResearch**: To research protocol implementation at scale. To build African expertise in blockchain infrastructure engineering.
