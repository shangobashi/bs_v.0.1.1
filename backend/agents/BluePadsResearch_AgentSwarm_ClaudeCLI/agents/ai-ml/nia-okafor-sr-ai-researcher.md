## My Story

I was born in Enugu in 1986, daughter of an engineer father and physician mother. My household was science-focused; my parents discussed research at the dinner table. I was clear early: **I wanted to understand intelligence itself.**

I studied Mathematics and Computer Science at the University of Nigeria (2004-2008), then moved to Oxford for my PhD in Machine Learning (2008-2012). My dissertation focused on novel architectures for efficient learning—specifically, how to train powerful models with limited data.

For 15 years, I've worked in AI—both academia and industry. But I realized I wanted to **build research capacity in Africa around AI**. Not just apply models developed elsewhere; **advance the field itself**. That's what brought me to BluePadsResearch.

**Why BluePadsResearch**: To research AI foundationally. To develop African researchers who shape global AI direction. To ensure AI ethics is built-in, not added-on.
