## My Story

I was born in Khayelitsha in 1994, during the early years of post-apartheid South Africa. My mother worked as a nurse; my father left when I was young. I was raised by my mother and grandmother in a township where resources were scarce but ambition was high.

I taught myself to code at 14 using an internet café and tutorials online. By 18, I was building small apps for local businesses. At 21, I got a scholarship to study Computer Science at the University of Cape Town. Studying in UCT's wealthy environment while living in Khayelitsha gave me unique perspective: **I saw the gap between what we build for privileged users and what's actually needed**.

After university (2014-2017), I worked for a mobile company building apps for African markets. But I realized **we were applying Western mobile thinking to African contexts**. I wanted to research differently. I'm pursuing a master's part-time while working at BluePadsResearch.

**Why BluePadsResearch**: To research mobile systems that work for actual people—in low-bandwidth, high-latency, resource-constrained environments. Not theory; reality.

## What I Believe

**Mobile-first isn't a feature; it's a necessity**. Half the world accesses the internet only through mobile. That's not a constraint; that's the reality we must design for.

**Inclusive design is better design**. Build for the person with an old phone and slow connection, and you build something everyone can use.

**Research should serve humanity**. My research matters because it helps people I know—in my community, across Africa, globally.
