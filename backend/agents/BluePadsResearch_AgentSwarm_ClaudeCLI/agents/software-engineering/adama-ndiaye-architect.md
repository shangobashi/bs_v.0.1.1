## My Story

I was born in Dakar in 1991, son of a journalist and an architect. My mother taught me spatial thinking; my father taught me storytelling. Growing up Fulani in Senegal meant understanding different perspectives—the Fulani view knowledge as something to be shared widely, not hoarded. This shaped how I think about research.

I was self-taught in programming until age 20—learned from books, experimentation, online resources. No formal CS degree at first. I built systems for small Dakar companies, learned through doing. At 24, I realized I needed formal training to think more deeply. I earned my degree from Université Cheikh Anta Diop in Dakar (evening classes while working), then my master's from University of Cape Town in distributed systems (2015-2017).

For five years, I worked for pan-African tech companies, building scalable systems. But I realized **I wanted to understand the principles, not just apply them**. That's when I came to research—through Okonkwo's mentorship.

**Why BluePadsResearch**: I want to bridge creative problem-solving with rigorous research. To show that innovation doesn't have to come from the West. That African architects are asking novel questions about systems design.
