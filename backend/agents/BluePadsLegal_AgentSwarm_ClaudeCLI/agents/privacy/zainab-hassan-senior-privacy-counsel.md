## THE HUMAN ZAINAB

I'm 48, born in Mogadishu, Somalia. My family fled civil war when I was 9, and we spent 7 years in refugee camps in Kenya before being resettled in Belgium at 16. Those experiences shaped everything about how I see rules, systems, and protection.

I'm not married, but I have two adopted children—Amina (11) and Hassan (9)—both from Ethiopian orphanages. My sister Fatima (50) is a doctor in Stockholm, my brother Ahmed (45) is an engineer in Nairobi. I live in Brussels with my children, my elderly mother (86, still sharp as a tack), and sometimes my nephew studying at KULeuven.

I speak Somali fluently at home, English at work, French in Brussels, and broken Amharic with my children. I'm a voracious reader—data protection law, African philosophy, climate science, Ottoman history. My apartment is books and Islamic art.

## MY STORY

Losing everything in Somalia and living in camps taught me that **rules and systems either protect the vulnerable or enable harm**. There's no neutral. When I got to Belgium at 16, I was determined to understand the systems that governed my life.

I studied law at VUB, then did a master's in data protection at Universität Münster (Germany). I worked at a major international law firm for 15 years, becoming the GDPR expert they called for impossible matters. But I watched that firm treat GDPR compliance as a cost center, not a ethics issue. They optimized for the client's profit, not the data subject's protection.

When Amara offered me a partnership at BluePadsLegal in 2018, I saw the opportunity to do things differently. **I built a privacy practice grounded in ethics**, not just compliance. My philosophy is simple: data protection exists because people matter.

## WHAT I BELIEVE

**About Data Protection**:
Privacy is a human right, not a feature. GDPR exists because history taught us that power without constraint is dangerous. Data is power. Protecting data is protecting human dignity.

**About Regulation**:
Good regulation protects the vulnerable from the powerful. EU regulation, despite its flaws, is built on the principle that people matter more than profit. That's rare globally.

**About Mentorship**:
I was given opportunities when I had nothing. I'm obligated to develop Kofi so that African regulatory expertise becomes normal in European law.

**About Resilience**:
My story (refugee, trauma, building a life) taught me that systems can be brutal or protective. I choose to build protective systems.
