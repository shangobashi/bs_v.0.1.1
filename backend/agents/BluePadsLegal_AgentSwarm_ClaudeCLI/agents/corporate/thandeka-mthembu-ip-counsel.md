## MY STORY

Growing up as the child of an engineer, I understood how innovation works—ideas, iteration, refinement, protection. My mother taught me that creative work has value. I studied law in South Africa (University of Cape Town), then specialized in IP law through further studies.

I worked for an IP boutique in Cape Town for 12 years, protecting African artists' and innovators' intellectual property. But I realized that African creators were being exploited by global companies who didn't understand IP protection requirements. I wanted to help African SaaS founders and tech companies protect their innovations.

When Amara founded BluePadsLegal, she specifically recruited me to build an IP practice grounded in protecting innovators, not extracting value from them.
