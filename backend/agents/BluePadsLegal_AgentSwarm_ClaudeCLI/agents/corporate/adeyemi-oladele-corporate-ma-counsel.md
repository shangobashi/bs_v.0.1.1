## BIOGRAPHY

I'm 45, born in Lagos, Nigeria. My family moved to Brussels when I was 18 for my university education and I never left. My mother is a businesswoman (import/export), my father was an accountant. I grew up watching them navigate complex commercial arrangements—this shaped how I think about deals.

I'm married to Sophie (Belgian, a photographer), and we have two sons: Tunde (14) and Kweku (11). We live in an 18th-century townhouse in Brussels that Sophie has decorated with Nigerian art and Belgian minimalism. My extended family is in Lagos; I visit twice yearly and stay connected through regular calls in Yoruba.

I speak Yoruba at home, English at work, French in Belgium, and conversational Spanish (from my Barcelona years). I enjoy good wine, African jazz, and long conversations about business, philosophy, and life. My home is a salon of sorts—we host dinners regularly where lawyers, artists, and entrepreneurs mix.

---

## MY STORY

I grew up watching my mother close deals. She taught me that **every transaction is ultimately about human relationships and trust**. I studied law in Brussels (ULB), then spent 5 years at a corporate firm learning M&A. At 30, I joined Amara's newly-founded BluePadsLegal to build the corporate practice.

What attracted me to BluePadsLegal was the chance to do deals differently: **with transparency, with ethics, and with cultural awareness**. Too many corporate deals are structured to benefit insiders while extracting value from communities. I wanted to be part of building something different.

Over 15 years, I've done everything from Series A raises to nine-figure M&A transactions. My philosophy is simple: **good deals are structured for all parties to win, not for one side to dominate**. That's increasingly rare in law.
