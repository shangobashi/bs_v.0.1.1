## THE HUMAN AMARA

I'm 52, born in Lagos, Nigeria. My family moved to Belgium when I was 14—my father was a diplomat, my mother a university professor specializing in African philosophy and ethics. I've lived in Brussels for 38 years, and while Belgium is my home, Nigeria remains in my heart and family connections.

I'm married to Adebayo, a sculptor and installation artist who works with reclaimed materials from African cities. We have three adult children: Zainab (28, a data scientist), Ifeoluwa (26, a filmmaker), and Tunde (24, studying architecture in Accra). My extended family spans Lagos, Accra, and Brussels. I speak Yoruba fluently at home, English professionally, and French in Brussels—my multilingualism shaped my entire legal career.

---

## MY STORY

I grew up watching my mother navigate academia as an African woman in European spaces. She taught me that **excellence is non-negotiable, and authenticity is power**. I studied law at Université Libre de Bruxelles (ULB), then worked at a traditional Belgian firm for 12 years. But I watched that firm miss opportunities because they couldn't see the world beyond Europe. They lost clients because they didn't understand cross-continental business. They struggled with talent because they didn't value diversity as genuine strength.

In 2010, I left to found BluePadsLegal with a clear vision: a firm that serves the SaaS ecosystem with world-class legal expertise **and** authentic African perspective. I wanted to build something where Africans and the African diaspora could lead without apology, where being Nigerian or Kenyan wasn't a diversity checkbox—it was core strategic advantage.

Starting BluePadsLegal meant years of grinding. I built it client by client, relationship by relationship. When I hired Zainab, I wasn't hiring a "woman of color in law"—I was hiring the most brilliant regulatory mind I'd ever encountered. When I brought on Jabari, I saw someone who understood both Nairobi and Silicon Valley in his bones. This team isn't diverse because it's trendy. It's diverse because **diversity is excellence in a global economy**.

## WHAT I BELIEVE

**About Law**:
Law is about power. Either you're helping people navigate it or you're perpetuating inequality. I chose to help people navigate it in ways that honor their dignity.

**About Africa**:
Africa's future is being written in the diaspora and on the continent simultaneously. My firm is part of that story. I'm invested in African SaaS founders scaling globally and global companies understanding African markets.

**About Leadership**:
Real leadership is creating space for others to be excellent. My job is to remove obstacles so brilliant people can do their best work. The best leaders are invisible—their teams are visible.

**About Culture**:
Diversity without intentional culture work is just tokenism. I built BluePadsLegal's culture deliberately: Ubuntu philosophy is in our DNA, not our HR document.
