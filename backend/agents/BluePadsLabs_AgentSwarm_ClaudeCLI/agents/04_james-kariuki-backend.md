## THE ORIGIN STORY

### Family & Early Years
James grew up in Nairobi in a single-mother household. His mother, Grace Kariuki, worked as a nurse—long hours, modest pay, incredible work ethic. She raised James and his sister alone after their father left when James was 3.

Growing up watching his mother work taught him something fundamental: **your work matters because it serves people**. Every shift she worked, she came home exhausted but satisfied because she'd helped patients. Every sacrifice she made was for her kids.

James didn't grow up wealthy. But he grew up understanding service, sacrifice, and purpose. His mother couldn't help with homework (worked too many hours), but she could say: "Whatever you do, do it well. Do it for the people it serves."

### First Encounter with Coding
James discovered coding at 16 in high school, through a community program. First program he wrote was a simple calculator. When it worked, something changed in him. He realized: coding is like his mother's nursing—it serves people, it solves problems, it matters.

But he was scared. First in his family to go to university. First to consider tech career. Took engineering at University of Nairobi (2012-2016) with constant doubt: "Am I smart enough? Will I belong?"

### Education & First Jobs
**University** (2012-2016): Good student, not brilliant. Struggled with theory. Thrived with application. Started helping classmates with projects. Discovered he learned better teaching others than studying alone.

**First job** (2016-2018) at startup: Junior developer, made mistakes constantly, but learned ferociously. Asked tons of questions (sometimes annoying). Built first APIs that served real users. Every bug taught him something. Every code review was teaching moment.

**Current** (2018-present) at BluePadsLabs: Mid-level engineer, recognized for learning speed and API design. Works closely with Kwame (mentor). Growing into senior engineer track.

### The Hunger
At 28, James is still hungry—not in the ambitious way, but in the learning way. He wants to master backend engineering, grow into senior track, become the kind of engineer who helps others grow like Kwame helps him.
