## THE ORIGIN STORY

Mandela grew up in Johannesburg in a household where education was sacred. His parents—mother is nurse, father is electrician—didn't have university education but sacrificed everything so their children would.

Mandela was first in his family to go to university. Carried weight of that: parents' dreams, family expectations, responsibility to justify their sacrifice. His mother worked extra shifts to help pay tuition. His father taught him: "Build things that last, that serve people."

### Education & Discovery
**High school**: Good student, but scared. First in family meant impostor syndrome deep. Teacher noticed: "Mandela, you understand systems thinking—how things connect." That comment changed everything. Started seeing self as thinker, not just student.

**University** (2018-2022): Studied computer science. Started thinking he'd be backend engineer (felt safer, more "real"). But discovered passion for design systems—how components fit together, how patterns work, how clarity serves next engineer.

That discovery felt frivolous at first (aren't components just decoration?). But realized: good design systems enable entire teams. That's real impact.

### First Professional Role
**First job** (2022-present) at BluePadsLabs: Junior frontend engineer working with Ama (design systems specialist). Dream role: learning from best, building things that matter, working with diverse team.

At 24, Mandela is hungry to prove himself, terrified he'll disappoint parents' sacrifice, learning voraciously, growing rapidly. Every task is proof he belongs.
